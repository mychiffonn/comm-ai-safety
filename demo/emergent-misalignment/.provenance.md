# Research provenance

## Scope and date

Research was conducted through July 18, 2026. The search universe was the publicly indexed web, including English and surfaced German, Spanish, Polish, Japanese, Chinese, Korean, and Russian items. The corpus includes direct press/news coverage, publisher and institutional releases, science-media-center reactions, commentary, and clearly labeled researcher/community communication. It separates originals, translations, syndications, aggregations, and adjacent uses.

## Search protocol

Queries combined:

- exact paper titles, `arXiv:2502.17424`, and DOI `10.1038/s41586-025-09937-5`;
- `Emergent Misalignment`, author names, and distinctive headline/output phrases;
- date windows around February–March 2025, June–September 2025, and January–March 2026;
- targeted searches of Nature, PMLR, university sites, Science Media Centres, technology and science outlets, LessWrong/Alignment Forum, X thread archives, podcasts, and video pages;
- multilingual equivalents and domain-targeted searches, followed by syndication tracing.

The authors' [project page](https://www.emergent-misalignment.com/) and [TruthfulAI news index](https://truthful.ai/news/) were used as discovery aids, not as proof of completeness or independent validation.

## Source roles

- **Primary technical authority:** PMLR/ICML paper, Nature paper, supplements, repository, and Zenodo archive.
- **Author-controlled communication:** project page, X threads, LessWrong/Alignment Forum author posts, talks, and sample browser.
- **Interested releases:** publisher, university, lab, or program communication.
- **Independent or commissioned editorial work:** newsroom reporting, features, expert commentary, and opinion, labeled by genre.
- **Derivative sources:** wire copies, licensed translations, aggregations, and AI-assisted summaries; these are deduplicated by source family.

## Completeness and access limits

The correct completeness statement is: **all coverage located under this documented, broad best-effort protocol**, not “all coverage everywhere.” Likely gaps include unindexed local coverage, deleted or login-only social posts, paywalled or robots-blocked full text, non-transcribed audio/video, newsletters without web archives, and languages not surfaced by the searches. French and Italian searches found no clearly substantive independent item.

Where only a snippet, translation, inaccessible page, or derivative copy was available, the audit uses `unverified` rather than treating the snippet as the full artifact. Syndicated copies are listed but not double-counted as independent reports.

## Tool fallback

The requested academic-paper research route was attempted, but the local `alpha` CLI was unavailable. The deep-research skill's referenced prompt file was also missing. Primary papers and authoritative web sources were therefore inspected directly, and the limitation is disclosed rather than silently replacing the source base.
